# Mahabharata References in Vedic Texts

## Overview

This document catalogs references to characters and dynasties associated with the Mahabharata epic found in earlier Vedic literature, including the Atharvaveda, Rigveda, Satapatha Brahmana, Yajurveda, and Apastamba Srautasutra. These references represent the historical substrate from which the Mahabharata narrative developed.

---

## 1. Parikshit (Parikshit)

### Atharvaveda 20.127.7-10 - Hymn to King Parikshit

**AV 20.127.7**

> **Sanskrit:** राज्ञो विश्वजनीनस्य यो देवोमर्त्यां अति । वैश्वानरस्य सुष्टुतिमा सुनोता परिक्षितः ॥
>
> **English:** "List to Parikshit's eulogy, the sovran whom all people love, The King who ruleth over all, excelling mortals as a God."

**AV 20.127.8**

> **Sanskrit:** परिछिन्नः क्षेमम् अकरोत् तम् आसनमाचरन् । कुलायन् कृण्वन् कौरव्यः पतिर् वदति जायया ॥
>
> **English:** "'Mounting his throne, Parikshit, best of all, hath given us peace and rest,' Saith a **Kauravya** to his wife as he is ordering his house."

_Note: The term **Kauravya** (member of Kuru clan) appears here!_

**AV 20.127.9**

> **Sanskrit:** कतरत् त आ हराणि दधि मन्थां परि श्रुतम् । जायाः पतिं वि पृछति राष्ट्रे राज्ञः परिक्षितः ॥
>
> **English:** "'Which shall I set before thee, curds, gruel of milk, or barley-brew?' Thus the wife asks her husband in the realm which King Parikshit rules."

**AV 20.127.10**

> **Sanskrit:** अभीवस्वः प्र जिहीते यवः पक्वः पथो बिलम् । जनः स भद्रम् एधति राष्ट्रे राज्ञः परिक्षितः ॥
>
> **English:** "Up as it were to heavenly light springs the ripe corn above the cleft. Happily thrive the people in the land where King Parikshit reigns."

## 2. Janamejaya Parikshita (जनमेजय पारिक्षित)

### Satapatha Brahmana 13.5.4.1 - The Asvamedha of Janamejaya

> **Sanskrit:** एतेन हेन्द्रोतो दैवापः शौनकः जनमेजयम् पारिक्षितं याजयां चकार तेनेष्ट्वा सर्वाम् पापकृत्यां सर्वाम् ब्रह्महत्यामपजघान
>
> **English:** "By this (sacrifice) Indrota Daivapa Saunaka performed the Asvamedha for Janamejaya Parikshita. Having performed it, he destroyed all his sinful deeds, all his brahmahatya (killing of Brahmins)."

> **सर्वां ह वै पापकृत्यां सर्वाम् ब्रह्महत्यामपहन्ति योऽश्वमेधेन यजते**
>
> "Whosoever performs the Asvamedha destroys all sinful deeds, all brahmahatya."

### Satapatha Brahmana 11.5.5.13 - The Palace of Janamejaya

> **Sanskrit:** सोमाः सोमैर्व्यतिषक्ताः प्लवन्ते समानान्त्सदमुक्षन्ति हयान्काष्ठभृतो यथा पूर्णान्परिस्रुतः कुम्भान् **जनमेजयसादने**
>
> **English:** "...streams of Soma flow, interlinked with streams of Soma!—Even as they constantly sprinkle the equal prize-winning steeds, so (they pour out) the cups full of fiery liquor in the palace of **Janamejaya**."

### Satapatha Brahmana 13.5.4.2 - Verse sung about Janamejaya

> **Sanskrit:** आसन्दीवति धान्यादं रुक्मिणं हरितस्रजम् । अबध्नादश्वं सारङ्गं देवेभ्यो जनमेजय
>
> **English:** "At Asandivat, Janamejaya bound for the gods the horse with golden trappings and green garlands, feeding on grain."

## 3. The Parikshitiyas - Sons of Parikshit

### Satapatha Brahmana 13.5.4.4

Three sons of Parikshit who performed Asvamedha sacrifices:

| Name      | Sanskrit | Sacrifice Type |
| --------- | -------- | -------------- |
| Bhimasena | भीमसेन   | Jyoti-Atiratra |
| Ugrasena  | उग्रसेन  | Gaur-Atiratra  |
| Srutasena | श्रुतसेन | Ayur-Atiratra  |

> **Sanskrit:** एते एव पूर्वे अहनी ज्योतिरतिरात्रस्तेन भीमसेनम् एते एव पूर्वे अहनी गौरतिरात्रस्तेनोग्रसेनम् एते एव पूर्वे अहनी आयुरतिरात्रस्तेन श्रुतसेनम् इत्येते पारिक्षितीयाः
>
> **Verse about them:** > **पारिक्षिता यजमाना अश्वमेधैः परोऽवरम् अजहुः कर्म पापकम् पुण्याः पुण्येन कर्मणा**
>
> **English:** "The Parikshitiyas, sacrificing with Asvamedhas, cast off evil deed after evil deed; pure they are through their pure deeds."

## 5. Amba, Ambika, Ambalika AND Subhadra (अम्बा, अम्बिका, अम्बालिका, सुभद्रा)

### Sukla Yajurveda (Vajasaneyi Samhita) 23.18 - Asvamedha Ritual

> **Sanskrit:** अम्बे अम्बिके म्बालिके न मा नयति कश्चन । ससस्त्यश्वकः **सुभद्रिकां काम्पीलवासिनीम्** ।।
>
> **English:** "Ambā! Ambikā! Ambālikā! No one is taking me away. The sorry horse will lie beside another, as **Subhadrā, the dweller in Kāmpīla**."

### Krishna Yajurveda (Taittiriya Samhita) 7.4.19

> **Sanskrit:** अम्बे अम्बाल्यम्बिके न मा नयति कश्चन । स सस्त्यश्वकः ॥ **सुभगे काम्पीलवासिनि** सुवर्गे लोके सं प्रोर्ण्वाथाम्
>
> **English:** "O Amba! O Ambali! O Ambika! No one leadest me. The wicked horse is sleeping. **O fair one (Subhage), dweller in Kampilya**, in the world of heaven be ye two covered."

### Satapatha Brahmana 13.2.8.3

> **Sanskrit:** अम्बे अम्बिके अम्बालिके न मा नयति कश्चन इति पत्नीरुदानयति
>
> **English:** "'Amba! Ambika! Ambalika! there is no one to lead me,' he leads up the (four) wives: he thereby has called upon them, and renders them sacrificially pure."

### Apastamba Srautasutra 20.18.3

> **Sanskrit:** सुभगे काम्पीलवासिनीति क्षौमेण वाससाध्वर्युर्महिषीमश्वं च प्रच्छाद्य
>
> **English:** "After the Adhvaryu has covered the chief queen and the horse by means of a linen garment with '**subhage kāmpīlavāsinī**' (O fortunate one, dweller in Kampilya)..."

**Significance:**

- **Ambā, Ambikā, Ambālikā** - The three princesses of Kashi in the Mahabharata
- **Subhadrā/Subhadrikā** (सुभद्रिका) - The very name that becomes Krishna's sister and Arjuna's wife in the Mahabharata!
- **Kāmpīlavāsinī** (काम्पीलवासिनी) - "Dweller in Kampilya" - the capital of the **Panchala** kingdom, from where **Draupadi** came!

_This Vedic mantra contains FOUR names associated with Mahabharata women in a single verse!_

## 6. Arjuna (अर्जुन)

### Satapatha Brahmana 5.4.3.7 - Arjuna as Indra's Secret Name

> **Sanskrit:** अरिष्टो अर्जुन इत्यर्जुनो ह वै नामेन्द्रो यदस्य गह्यं नाम द्वयेन वा एष इन्द्रो भवति यच्च क्षत्रियो यदु च यजमानः
>
> **English:** "'Uninjured Arjuna'—for Arjuna is indeed Indra's secret name. In two ways this one becomes Indra: both as a Kshatriya and as a Sacrificer."

### Sukla Yajurveda 9.6

> **English:** "I, the uninjured **Arjuna**, mount thee for firmness, thee for food. By quickening of the Maruts be thou victor."

## 7. "Where Have the Parikshitas Gone?"

### Satapatha Brahmana 14.6.3.1 (Brihadaranyaka Upanishad Section)

The famous philosophical question asked by Bhujyu Lahyayani to Yajnavalkya:

> **Sanskrit:** क्व पारिक्षिता अभवन् क्व पारिक्षिता अभवन्न्
>
> **English:** "Where have the Parikshitas gone? Where have the Parikshitas gone?"

_This question—about where the great Parikshit kings went after death—represents one of the earliest philosophical inquiries into the fate of souls after death._

## 8. Kuru Dynasty (कुरु)

### Apastamba Srautasutra 20.18.8 - Royal Announcements

> **English:** "Then (the Adhvaryu) informs about (the sacrificer) to the Ratnins:
>
> - If belonging to the **Kuru**-family: 'This is your king, O Bharatas!'
> - If belonging to **Panchalas**: 'This is your king, O Kurus!'
> - If belonging to **Kuru-Panchala** family: 'This is your king, O Kuru-Panchalas!'
> - If belonging to any other family: 'This is your king, O people!'"

_This shows the historical reality of the Kuru-Panchala kingdoms as major powers._

### Rigveda 4.15.7-10 - Praise of Sahadeva's Son

**RV 4.15.7**

> **English:** "When **Sahadeva's** princely son with two bay horses thought of me, Summoned by him I drew not back."

**RV 4.15.8**

> **English:** "And truly those two noble bays I straightway took when offered me, From **Sahadeva's** princely son."

**RV 4.15.9**

> **English:** "Long, O ye Asvins, may he live, your care, ye Gods, the princely son Of **Sahadeva**, Somaka."

**RV 4.15.10**

> **English:** "Cause him the youthful prince, the son of **Sahadeva**, to enjoy Long life, O Asvins, O ye Gods."

## 10. Bhishma (भीष्म)

### Satapatha Brahmana 12.9.3.1-4 - The Word "Bhishma" (Terrible)

In the story of Bhrigu's journey through the worlds:

> **Sanskrit:** स होवाच भीष्मम् बत भोः पुरुषान्न्वा एतत्पुरुषाः पर्वाणयेषां संव्रश्चम् पर्वशो व्यभक्षत
>
> **English:** "He said, 'Terrible indeed (bhishmam)! Men are devouring men, cutting them limb from limb!'"

### Satapatha Brahmana 13.2.2.6 - Army Front Called Bhishma

> **Sanskrit:** सेनामुखमेवास्यैतेन संश्यति तस्माद्राज्ञः सेनामुखम् भीष्मम् भावुकम्
>
> **English:** "...thereby he sharpens the front of his army, whence the front of the king's army is sure to become **bhishma** (terrible/formidable)."

## Significance

These references demonstrate that:

1. **Parikshit** was a historical king praised in Vedic hymns, not merely a legendary figure
2. **Janamejaya's Asvamedha** to expiate brahmahatya is recorded in Brahmana literature
3. The **Kuru-Panchala** kingdoms were major political entities in Vedic times
4. Names like **Amba, Ambika, Ambalika** existed in ritual contexts before the epic
5. **Arjuna** was associated with Indra as a secret/sacred name
6. The philosophical question "Where have the Parikshitas gone?" shows these kings were remembered as exemplars

These Vedic references represent the historical substrate from which the Mahabharata narrative developed over centuries.
