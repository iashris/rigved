The erotic/devotional Krishna isn't a single tradition. It's at least four separate regional streams, each with a different "consort" and a different mood of worship. And they map onto geography almost perfectly:

Stream 1: The South — Andal and the Alvars (6th-9th century CE)

This is where the erotic devotion to Krishna actually begins. Not Bengal. Not Vrindavan. Tamil Nadu. The Alvar poet-saints — especially Andal (8th-9th century) and Nammalvar — wrote intensely erotic poetry addressed to Vishnu/Krishna. Male Alvars like Nammalvar adopted the female voice — the lovelorn woman pining for her absent divine lover. This is called viraha bhakti — devotion through the agony of separation.

But here's the critical thing: they don't have Radha. There's no Radha in the Alvar tradition. The devotee herself becomes the yearning woman.
