## WHITE YAJURVEDA (Shukla Yajurveda / Vajasaneyi Samhita)

### **Verse 16.28 - Homage to Craftsmen**

**Sanskrit:**
```sanskrit
नमस्तक्षभ्यो रथकारेभ्यश्च वो नमो नमः कुलालेभ्यः कुर्मारेभ्यश्च वो नमो
नमो निषादेभ्यः पुञ्जिष्टेभ्यश्च वो नमो नमः श्वनिभ्यो मृगयुभ्यश्च वो नमः ।।
```

**Translation:**
"Homage to you carpenters, and to you chariot-makers homage.
Homage to you potters and to you blacksmiths, homage.
Homage to you Nishâdas and to you Puñjishthas, homage.
Homage to you dog-leaders, and to you hunters, homage."

**Professions Mentioned:**
- **तक्ष (taksha)** - Carpenters
- **रथकार (rathakāra)** - Chariot-makers
- **कुलाल (kulāla)** - Potters
- **कुर्मार/कर्मार (kurmāra/karmāra)** - Blacksmiths
- **निषाद (niṣāda)** - Nishadas (hunting/fishing tribe)
- **पुञ्जिष्ट (puñjiṣṭa)** - Puñjishthas (hunting people)
- **श्वनिन् (śvanin)** - Dog-leaders
- **मृगयु (mṛgayu)** - Hunters

### **Verse 30.6 - Professions for Various Qualities**

**Sanskrit:**
```sanskrit
नृत्ताय सूतं गीताय शैलूषं धर्माय सभाचरं नरिष्ठायै भीमलं
नर्माय रेभँ हसाय कारिमाननदाय स्त्रीसुखं प्रमदे कुमारीपुत्रं
मेधायै रथकारं धैर्याय तक्षाणम् ।।
```

**Translation:**
"For Dance a stable-master; for Song a public dancer;
for Duty one who attends court; for Pastime a timid man;
for Sport a chatterer; for Laughter an artist;
for Pleasure a woman-lover; for Desire a damsel's son;
for Dexterity a car-builder; for Firmness a carpenter;"

**Professions Mentioned:**
- **सूत (sūta)** - Stable-master/charioteer
- **शैलूष (śailūṣa)** - Public dancer/actor
- **सभाचर (sabhācara)** - Court attendant
- **कारि (kāri)** - Artist/craftsman
- **रथकार (rathakāra)** - Car-builder/chariot-maker
- **तक्षन् (takṣan)** - Carpenter

### **Verse 30.14 - Metal Workers and Craftswomen**

**Sanskrit:**
```sanskrit
मन्यवे यस्तापं क्रोधाय निसरं योगाय योक्तारँ शोकायाभिसर्तारं
क्षेमाय विमोक्तारं आर्ध्वाधोर्वे त्रिपदं वपुषे मानस्कृतँ
शीलायाञ्जनीकारीं निरृत्यै कोशकारीं यमायासूम् ।।
```

**Translation:**
"For Passion an iron-smelter; for Anger a remover;
for Yoking a yoker; for Pain an assailant; for Quiet an unyoker;
for Up-hill and Down-hill one who stands on three legs;
for Form a conceited man; for Virtue a female ointment-maker;
for Nirriti a female scabbard-maker; for Yama a barren woman;"

**Professions Mentioned:**
- **अयस्ताप (ayastāpa)** - Iron-smelter (partial match) ✓
- **अञ्जनीकारी (añjanīkārī)** - Female ointment/cosmetics-maker ✓
- **कोशकारी (kośakārī)** - Female scabbard/box-maker ✓

## BLACK YAJURVEDA (Krishna Yajurveda / Taittiriya Samhita)

### **Verse 4.5.4 - Homage to Various Professions**

**Sanskrit:**
```sanskrit
नम आव्याधिनीभ्यो विविध्यन्तीभ्यश्च वो नमो नम उगणाभ्यस्तृँहतीभ्यश्च वो नमो
नमो गृत्सेभ्यो गृत्सपतिभ्यश्च वो नमो नमो व्रातेभ्यो व्रातपतिभ्यश्च वो नमो
नमो गणेभ्यो गणपतिभ्यश्च वो नमो नमो विरूपेभ्यो विश्वरूपेभ्यश्च वो नमो
नमो महद्भ्यः क्षुल्लकेभ्यश्च वो नमो नमो रथिभ्योऽरथेभ्यश्च वो नमो
नमो रथेभ्यो रथपतिभ्यश्च वो नमो नमः सेनाभ्यः सेनानिभ्यश्च वो नमो
नमः क्षत्तृभ्यः सङ्ग्रहीतृभ्यश्च वो नमो नमस्तक्षभ्यो रथकारेभ्यश्च वो नमो
नमः कुलालेभ्यः कर्मारेभ्यश्च वो नमो नमः पुञ्जिष्टेभ्यो निषादेभ्यश्च वो नमो
नम इषुकृद्भ्यो धन्वकृद्भ्यश्च वो नमो नमो मृगयुभ्यः श्वनिभ्यश्च वो ॥
```

**Translation:**
"Homage to you that wound, and to you that pierce homage!
Homage to you that are in bands, and to you that are destructive homage!
Homage to you sharpers, and to you, lords of sharpers, homage!
Homage to you hosts, and to you, lord of hosts, homage!
Homage to you troops, and to you, lords of troops, homage
Homage to you of misshapen form, and to you of all forms homage!
Homage to you that are great, and to you that are small homage!
Homage to you that have chariots, and to you that are chariotless homage!
Homage to you chariots, and to you, lords of chariots, homage!
Homage to you hosts, and to you, lords of hosts, homage!
Homage to you, doorkeepers, and to you, charioteers, homage!
Homage to you, carpenters, and to you, makers of chariots, homage!
Homage to you, potters, and to you, smiths, homage!
Homage to you, Puñjistas, and to you, Nisadas, homage!
Homage to you, makers of arrows, and to you, makers of bows, homage!
Homage to you, hunters, and to you, dog-leaders, homage!
Homage to you dogs, and to you, lords of dogs, homage!"

**Professions Mentioned:**
- **क्षत्तृ (kṣattṛ)** - Doorkeepers ✓
- **सङ्ग्रहीतृ (saṅgrahītṛ)** - Charioteers
- **तक्ष (takṣa)** - Carpenters
- **रथकार (rathakāra)** - Makers of chariots
- **कुलाल (kulāla)** - Potters
- **कर्मार (karmāra)** - Smiths/blacksmiths
- **पुञ्जिष्ट (puñjiṣṭa)** - Puñjistas (hunting people)
- **निषाद (niṣāda)** - Nisadas (hunter-fishers)
- **इषुकृत् (iṣukṛt)** - Makers of arrows
- **धन्वकृत् (dhanvakṛt)** - Makers of bows
- **मृगयु (mṛgayu)** - Hunters
- **श्वनिन् (śvanin)** - Dog-leaders

## NOTES

1. The White Yajurveda contains significantly more profession references than the Black Yajurveda.

2. Chapter 30 of the White Yajurveda appears to be a comprehensive ritual listing of social roles and professions.

3. Many professions are mentioned in ritual contexts, associating different occupations with specific deities or qualities.

4. The Black Yajurveda's verse 4.5.4 closely parallels White Yajurveda's verse 16.28, suggesting these were important standard listings.

5. Some professions appear with gender-specific forms (ending in -ī for women), particularly in craft and service occupations.

6. Several professions from the original query may be found in other Vedic texts (Rigveda, Atharvaveda, Samaveda) or later Sanskrit literature.
