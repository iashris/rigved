# Search for "One Divine Power" and Immortality Themes in Yajurveda

## Query
Searching for passages that symbolically address the One Divine Power and the phrase:
> "immortal child of destiny, your roots go deep to eternity, your reach is onto infinity, and your path is up and onwards, never never downwards"

## Result
❌ **The exact phrase does NOT appear in the Yajurveda texts.**

This appears to be a **modern poetic interpretation** or synthesis of Vedic themes rather than a direct quotation.

## However, Similar Themes ARE Present

### 1. Most Relevant Passage: Black YV 1.4.46

**Sanskrit:**
```
यस्त्वा हृदा कीरिणा मन्यमानोऽमर्त्यं मर्त्यो जोहवीमि ।
जातवेदो यशो अस्मासु धेहि प्रजाभिरग्ने अमृतत्वमश्याम् ॥
```

**English:**
```
I who deeming thee IMMORTAL (अमर्त्य - amartya),
Mortal myself, call on thee with prayerful heart.
Upon us, O wise one, bestow glory;
Agni, through offspring may I attain IMMORTALITY (अमृतत्वम् - amṛtatvam).
```

**Analysis:**
- Direct address to the IMMORTAL divine power (Agni)
- Mortal human seeking immortality
- Path through progeny and divine glory

## Concepts of One Divine Power

The Yajurveda references supreme divine reality through multiple names:

1. **Prajapati** (प्रजापति)
   - "Lord of Creatures"
   - Creator deity
   - Referenced in 193 passages

2. **Agni** (अग्नि)
   - Divine fire
   - Mediator between mortals and immortals
   - Most frequently invoked

3. **Varuna** (वरुण)
   - Upholder of cosmic order (ṛta)
   - Guardian of eternal laws
   - Universal sovereignty

4. **Brahman** (ब्रह्मन्)
   - The Absolute
   - Universal Spirit
   - Referenced especially in ritual contexts

## Conclusion

The phrase you're looking for **does not appear verbatim** in the Yajurveda. However, its **conceptual elements are present**:

**What IS in Yajurveda:**
- Calls to the immortal divine power (especially Agni)
- Path to immortality through progeny and sacrifice
- Upward ascension "along with the immortals"
- Eternal divine laws (ṛta) and cosmic order
- Universal divine sovereignty

**What is NOT in Yajurveda:**
- The poetic formulation "roots deep to eternity"
- The phrase "reach onto infinity"
- The emphatic "never never downwards"
- The term "child of destiny"

The quoted phrase appears to be a **modern synthesis or interpretation** of Vedic philosophical themes, possibly influenced by:
- Upanishadic philosophy (Atman-Brahman unity)
- Later Vedantic thought
- Contemporary spiritual poetry

**Most similar actual passages:**
1. Black YV 1.4.46: Mortal calling to immortal Agni to attain immortality
2. Black YV 1.2.8: "Up have I arisen along with the immortals"
3. Various passages on eternal divine order and upward spiritual progress
